1.1 Describing the purpose of IT in modern business

IT's role in modern business is wide and varied, with many businesses unable to function in its absence. The internet allows organisations to be in touch with their audience, this sort of communication is vital for marketing and helps companies build their brand and reputation.

IT has innumerable practical applications in modern business, such as managing data, making calculations and facilitating connectivity between team members. The benefits of this connectivity allow for better time management and overall productivity.

[https://businesscasestudies.co.uk/the-use-of-it/]

A range of skills are required in order to successfully complete digital projects. 1.4 Describe the risks that might impact digital projects – what causes digital projects to fail? Why?

Risks that can impact digital projects and cause their failure include: poor planning, inadequate management, lack of leadership, poor communication, lack of user testing/failure to implement user feedback, poorly defined outcomes/failure to solve the problem presented.

[https://www.forbes.com/sites/bernardmarr/2016/09/13/are-these-the-real-reasons-why-tech-projects-fail/#2723353b7320]

Without addressing these risks, a digital project's likelihood to fail is increased. The project could suffer from poor time management and run over-budget without delivering the intended outcome.

[https://www.interruptdelivers.com/themash/what-is-risk-in-a-digital-project-and-why-should-i-care]

It is therefore vitally important that all digital projects should be effectively managed, providing accountabilty and structure for those working towards its completion.

1.5 Describe how you would go about selecting and using IT systems and software – how do you select the right kind of technology for the job?

It is important to ensure that IT systems and software selected to complete projects is fit for purpose. Researching different IT solutions for individual projects allows an organisation to select the best fit for the problem that needs to be solved. Some limitations may be caused by lack of budget, with certain platforms restricting features in free versions of their product. For example, free use of Slack only allows a team's last 10,000 messages to be accessed. Depending on the size of your team and timeline of the project, this allowance may not be adequate.

1.6 Describe and provide analysis on how your chosen technologies have helped you achieve your outcomes. – How has Trello helped you plan and how can git and GitHub help with your codes version control

Using Trello and the Kanban method has improved my ability to multi-task, making working on several different tasks at once easier. The visual interface of the card system allows at-a-glance progress updates, making dipping in and out of different tasks more manageable and presenting each individual task in bite-sized chunks. This impacts the overall productivity and easily communicates where a project is up to.

GitHub is a platform that allows developers to share code they've been working on, it streamlines a team's efforts, ensure work is not duplicated and provides version control. These factors improve overall productivity. Version control is a useful tool when working as part of a wider team, in the event changes/errors occur within the code, problems can be easily rectified by reverting to an earlier version.

1.7 Describe legal guidelines and constraints that impact digital projects – How does GDPR impact digital projects?

GDPR is a progressive approach to how people's personal data should be handled, [https://www.wired.co.uk/article/what-is-gdpr-uk-eu-legislation-compliance-summary-fines-2018] . It provides a legal framework and guidelines for the collecting and processing of personal information belonging to those living within the European Union (EU).

Failure to comply with the legislation set out in GDPR can result in businesses receiving hefty fines, which impacts an organisation's financial stability and their reputation. [https://gdpr-info.eu/issues/fines-penalties/]

Working as part of a team would have made a massive difference in terms of both productivity and efficiency. As a member of a team I would have been able to draw upon the knowledge and strengths of others. I would have spent less time researching the right code to use for specific elements of my website. A bigger team would have also allowed for more to have been produced within the given deadline. Consequently the website project suffers as a result of the restrictions. Larger teams would potentially have a greater capacity to produce a slicker, more powerful finished product.

Working alone has given me greater creative freedom to produce a design and layout of own choosing. I have benefited from the time I've saved consulting with others about aspects of the website build. This has allowed extra time to utilise online resources and practice new techniques.

3.2 Describe ways to improve productivity and efficiency

A good way to improve productivity and efficiency is by remembering to take regular breaks. Remembering to take breaks is both both physically and psychologically important. A problem might be easier to solve after have some time away from it and regular breaks are also important for health, helping avoid problems with eyesight/eyestrain.

I continued to use Trello for my own personal reference during the website project, as a way of monitoring progress with different tasks and keeping track of the project. This allowed me to evaluate the workload and better manage my time. By creating a 'backlog' column as part of the kanban I was better able to prioritise and establish which tasks were more important verses nice to have.

In future I would make better use of the colour coding feature on when using Trello. During the website project I spent time adding a some colours to the cards. By using this method in future I could better understand what a task involves at a glance.

A designer should test their website on multiple browsers throughout their build to minimise compatibility issues for users.
